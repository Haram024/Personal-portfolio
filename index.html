About Me

I am a computer engineering student inspired by seeing women excel in technology and driven by a curiosity to understand how systems work. Growing up in Islamabad, Pakistan, I became fascinated with exploring devices, electronics projects, and building small systems, which motivated me to pursue a career in computing and emerging technologies.

Beyond academics, I have cultivated strong skills in communication, presentations, and leadership, enabling me to articulate complex ideas effectively and guide teams toward successful outcomes.

My academic focus lies in computer engineering, machine learning, and AI-driven optimization. I have contributed to a range of projects, including traffic signal optimization with CSP and simulated annealing, predictive healthcare systems for diabetes, heart disease, and cancer using neural networks, and multi-band GPS signal modeling and transmission via software-defined radio. These experiences have strengthened my analytical abilities and reinforced my belief in technology as a force for meaningful impact. Motivated by a desire to create value and drive innovation, I continue to explore interdisciplinary research, emerging technologies, and practical solutions that can advance knowledge and benefit society.
